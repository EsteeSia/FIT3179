Relationship between Maternal Deaths and Maternal Mortality Rate

Majority of the states exhibit a positive relationship between the number of maternal deaths and the maternal mortality rate. For instance, Selangor exhibit a strong positive relationship between the two, where a higher number of maternal deaths corresponds to a higher maternal mortality rate. Notably, in 2021, Selangor shows a significant spike in both deaths and mortality rate, likely due to the impact of COVID-19.

The maternal mortality rate is calculated by number of maternal deaths per 100,000 live births.

Maternal Mortality Rate by State over Time

In 2021, W.P. Labuan recorded an alarming maternal mortality rate of 328.50, indicating a critical need for more healthcare interventions and resources.

This dataset excludes unregistered maternal deaths, but adjustments have been made in Sabah to address under-reporting. Additionally, W.P. Putrajaya has data available only from 2010 to 2022, which is an important consideration when comparing across states and years.
